## GO / NO-GO

**GO gate:** does pre-RoPE per-channel K get E2B 2-bit K to the TQ3 anchor (softmax-KL ~0.055, gap_closure -> 1.0), or at least clearly beat BOTH the post-RoPE per-channel 0.2488 AND the post-RoPE baseline 0.1869, at <= 2.5 bpe?

- **Bare pre-RoPE per-channel (the KIVI premise), `preB mse g1 (2b)` = 0.2251** (global 0.1868, local 0.2347) at 2.022 bpe. vs post-RoPE per-channel 0.2488: BEATS it (+9.5%). vs post-RoPE baseline 0.1869: does NOT beat it (-20.4%). Moving the per-channel scale into pre-RoPE space, where the outlier channels are intact, DOES help relative to post-RoPE per-channel — the KVQuant/KIVI premise directionally holds.
- **Best pre-RoPE 2-bit arm `PRE per-chan mse g1 + 2% outliers (2b)` = 0.0981** (global 0.0600, local 0.1077) at 2.506 bpe, gap_closure +0.674 — sits ~1.8x above the TQ3 anchor (0.055).
- Larger channel groups hurt (g8=0.3239, g32=0.3653) as in the post-RoPE study.

### Verdict: **NO-GO**

No pre-RoPE 2-bit arm reaches the TQ3 anchor (best gap_closure +0.674, 1.8x above 0.055). Moving per-channel quantization into pre-RoPE space modestly beats the post-RoPE per-channel arm (0.2251 vs 0.2488) (though still above the per-vector Hadamard baseline 0.1869), but the gap to a deployable 2-bit K is not closed. After the ~3-5x closed-loop amplification (VQ study rule 1) this still lands above the ~0.27 cliff, so 2-bit K would still collapse in the loop.

**Recommendation.** Pre-RoPE per-channel is a real-but-insufficient improvement for E2B 2-bit K; E2B K stays **TQ4 / gated-TQ3**. A store-pre-RoPE cache would recover the post-RoPE per-channel regression, but since even that does not reach a deployable 2-bit K, it does not justify the fused dequant+RoPE kernel + cache relayout on its own. The only cost-justified 2-bit lever remains a generic sparse-outlier side channel on the CURRENT post-RoPE per-vector Hadamard codec (see the sibling study).
